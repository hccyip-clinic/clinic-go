# Define the bilingual receipt printout format

## Question

Which receipt fields must print with English and Traditional Chinese paired labels, in what order and visual arrangement, while keeping the receipt clear, legally useful, and readable on paper?

## Resolution

Use an A4 portrait receipt with English first and Traditional Chinese paired in the same label, such as `Patient Name / 病人姓名`, rather than separate language columns. Pair every human-facing field label, including clinic details, receipt metadata, patient and visit details, diagnosis, treatment, quantity, prices, discount, total, and practitioner information. Keep values exactly as configured or entered, allowing English or Traditional Chinese clinic values without forced translation.

Order the printed sections as clinic identity and contact details, receipt metadata, patient and visit details, treatment line items, discount and grand total, then practitioner/signature and required footer notes.
